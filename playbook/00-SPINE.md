# 00-SPINE — the ROV Sales Engine

The part every vertical sheet inherits. Written once here so no sheet re-explains it.
A vertical sheet only holds what is *different* about that target: its ICP, its three
money leaks, its scripts. Everything below is shared.

**One job:** start conversations. Get a reply, a demo opened, or a call booked.
Not closing, not quoting. When someone bites, it becomes a call.

**Golden rule:** one wedge per lead, then expand. Never open with everything ROV does.
Lead with the single obvious gap for that vertical. Branding, automation, and media
only come up *after* they are interested.

---

## The four offers (what we actually sell)

Every vertical sheet frames the offer as **three engines** (name the target's three
money leaks, map each to what we sell). The engines are always built from these four:

- **Branding** — logo, look, consistency, the name they own
- **Website** — the site itself, built to convert
- **Automation** — missed-call text-back, follow-ups, review requests, reactivation, dashboard
- **Media** — photo, video, Reels, content that shows the work

**The package (only on the call, never in message 1):** $750 build + 30 days free +
$1K/mo, 3-month minimum. Always frame it against the dollar leak: "you're losing
$X/mo, this is $1K/mo."

You sell ONE engine per lead. The other two are the upside you reveal once they bite.

## Channel by size (the general rule)

Small and owner-operated lives on Instagram. Bigger and more official lives on email.
Home-services owners answer their phone. Real estate and B2B respond on LinkedIn.
Each sheet tightens this for its vertical.

| Size / type | Channel |
|---|---|
| Solo / mom & pop (owner lives on IG) | **Instagram DM** |
| Small team (2-10) | **IG first, email as backup** |
| Established / multi-location | **Email primary** (official, forwardable) |

**Send timing principle:** hit the dead hour when the owner is idle but reachable.
Tue-Thu, mid-day, never weekends. Each sheet sets the exact window (a restaurant's
dead hour is 2-4pm between services; an agent's is 10-2 between showings).

## The three angles (A/B/C — every sheet A/B/C tests these)

- **A — Diagnosis:** name the money leaks up front, offer to walk them through what
  you're seeing. Direct. Best when you want to lead with the problem.
- **B — Curiosity:** proof + a light tease, make them ask. Smoothest, cheapest to send
  at scale. The default for cold volume.
- **C — Show them something:** you already built the thing (mockup, Reel, audit) and
  want to walk them through it. Strongest, but only for a hand-picked short list,
  because you build the asset *first*. Never promise one you haven't made.

**CTA throughline:** "want me to walk you through it?" It implies you've already
looked, and it's an offer, not a demand. On reply, the reveal *is* the walkthrough:
the diagnosis or the built asset, depending on the angle you led with.

## Voice rules (this is what "copywriting" means here)

- **Sound human, not like a brochure.** Real name + one specific detail so it's
  obviously not a blast.
- **Never "would love to" / "happy to."** You're a peer who makes the thing they need.
  Use "want me to."
- **No em dashes.** Ever. Commas, periods, parentheses.
- **No formulaic "if you X then Y" constructions.**
- **Speak their language, not vendor-speak.** Use the words the target uses. Never say
  "leads / funnels / CRM / nurture" to people who say "sphere / listings / referrals."
  Vendor words read as commodity. Each sheet lists its allowed and banned vocabulary.
- **Name a villain that isn't them.** The portal (Zillow), the platform tax (DoorDash's
  30%), the template everyone else uses. You're on their side against it.
- **Proof is the intro.** Don't explain who ROV is in message 1. Lead with the result.
  Save the studio pitch for the reply.
- **One number everywhere.** Lock the proof stat and use the exact same figure in every
  message. Never invent a stat. No case study yet in a vertical = the built asset (Reel,
  mockup) is your proof instead.

## The close (show, don't tell)

We don't argue, we show. Two tools depending on the lead: **walk them through where
they're losing money right now**, or **build a quick asset** (mockup / Reel / audit)
so they see it before they pay. Either way it flips "should I?" into "how do we fix it?"

---

## Objection cheat sheet

- **"How much?"** → "Depends what you need, that's a quick call. The sample/audit is
  free either way." Do not quote a price cold.
- **"We already have someone / a site."** → "Totally fair. Mind if I send the sample
  anyway? If it's better than what you've got, great. If not, no harm."
- **"Not interested."** → "No problem. If it helps, here's the free sample to keep."
  Then mark dead.
- **"Send me info."** → send the asset or a one-line recap, mark contacted, follow up
  in 3 days.
- **"Too expensive."** → tie it to the dollar leak. It pays for itself.
- **"Let me think."** → one low-friction next step (pick a launch date), not a beg.

---

## Tracking discipline (do not skip)

Every touch gets logged: **Contacted** on reach-out, **Interested** on a warm reply,
**Client** on signing, **Dead** on a no. One-line note each ("called, wants quote
Friday"). A lead you worked but didn't log is a lead you wasted.
