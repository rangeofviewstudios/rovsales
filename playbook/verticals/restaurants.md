# Restaurants — Sales Sheet

**Who:** Independent restaurants, coffee shops & cafés — metro Atlanta. 1-5 locations, owner-operated. Buyer is the owner (fast yes/no).
**Skip:** National chains, franchises, corporate marketing depts, ghost kitchens. No local decision-maker = no deal.

---

## The Offer — *Get Seen → Get Chosen → Get Kept*

The food is the best part of the business, and it's quietly losing money before anyone ever tastes it. Money leaks at three stages: people searching for a place to eat never find them, the ones who do land on a site too rough to order from, and the orders they do get, they hand 30% to DoorDash. We plug all three.

| Step | What it does | The money it saves |
|---|---|---|
| **Get Seen** | Scroll-stopping photo/video + **SEO so you show up when people search Google** | People searching for dinner never find you = lost customers |
| **Get Chosen** | Website as good as the food, one clear path to order | Find you but bounce off an ugly site/PDF menu = lost orders |
| **Get Kept** | Own your customers — reviews, email/SMS, direct ordering, an audience you actually own | DoorDash takes 30% and keeps the customer = lost margin + no repeats |

**Proof:** We doubled @thebandoatl's online revenue in about 3 months.

**The close (show, don't tell):** We don't argue, we show them. Two tools depending on the lead: **walk them through where they're losing money right now** (invisible on Google, bouncing off the site, DoorDash eating 30%), or **build a quick mockup** of what their new site could look like. Either way they see it before they pay, and it flips from "should I?" to "how do we fix it?"

**Bar test:** *"Restaurants have unreal food, and they're losing money before anyone tastes it. People can't find them, the ones who do don't order, and the orders they get, they hand 30% to DoorDash. We plug all three and build them an audience they own. We doubled one spot's online revenue in 3 months, and we'll walk you through exactly where you're losing money right now."*

## Which channel to use (by size)

| Size | Channel |
|---|---|
| 1 location (mom & pop) | **Instagram DM** — owner lives there |
| 2-3 locations | **IG first, email as backup** |
| 4-5 locations | **Email primary** — more official, forwardable |

**Send timing:** Tue-Thu, **2-4 PM** (dead hour between lunch and dinner). Never Fri/Sat. Many spots closed Mon/Tue.

---

## The three angles to A/B/C test

- **A — Diagnosis:** name the money leaks, offer to walk them through what you're seeing. Direct, best when you want to lead with the problem.
- **B — Curiosity:** proof + a light tease, make them ask. Smoothest, cheapest to send at scale.
- **C — Mockup:** you already built something, want to walk them through it. Strongest but only for a hand-picked short list (you build the mockup first).

CTA throughline: **"want me to walk you through it / them / what I'm seeing?"** — it implies you've already looked, and it's an offer, not a demand.

## Rules of the road
- **Play:** B for cold volume (curiosity, cheap to send), A when you want to name the leak up front, C for a hand-picked short list where you build the mockup first. On reply, the reveal is the walkthrough — the diagnosis or the mockup, depending on the angle you led with.
- Never say "would love to" / "happy to" — you're a peer who just doubled a restaurant's revenue. Use "want me to."
- Don't explain who ROV is in message 1. The Bando result is the intro. Save the studio pitch for the reply.

*Proof note (Suchet): confirm and lock the exact Bando figure before wide send — keep one number everywhere.*
