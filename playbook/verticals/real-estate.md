# Real Estate Agents — Sales Sheet

**Who:** Residential agents who reinvest in themselves, metro Atlanta. Top producers, boutique teams (3-10 agents), agents with a real budget. The agent IS the business, so the buyer is the agent or team lead (fast yes/no).
**Skip:** Brand-new agents with zero budget, anyone who leans 100% on the brokerage's marketing and won't spend on their own name, corporate brokerage marketing departments.

## Which channel to use (by type)

| Type | Channel |
|---|---|
| Solo agent (lives on IG) | **Instagram DM** — where they already are all day |
| Boutique team (3-10) | **IG first, email as backup** |

**Send timing:** Tue-Thu, **10 AM to 2 PM**. Avoid weekends (open houses and showings) and Monday mornings.

## The three angles to A/B/C test

- **A — Diagnosis:** name the two money leaks (renting Zillow leads + dead database), offer to walk them through it. Direct, best when you want to lead with the problem.
- **B — Curiosity:** tease the referral money sitting in their sphere, make them ask. Smoothest, cheapest to send at scale.
- **C — Reel:** you already cut a Reel of their listing and want to send it over. Strongest, but only for a hand-picked short list (you build the Reel first).

CTA throughline: **"want me to walk you through it?"** (diagnosis/database) or **"want me to send it over?"** (Reel). It's an offer, not a demand.

## Rules of the road
- **Play:** C (the Reel) is your strongest angle here, and you should lean on it more than in other verticals. You have no real estate case study yet, so a Reel of their own listing sidesteps the "who have you done this for?" question completely. Use B for cold volume, A when you want to name the money leak up front. On reply, the reveal is the walkthrough or the Reel, depending on the angle you led with.
- **The Reel angle requires you to actually cut the Reel first.** Never promise one you haven't made. Build it for a hand-picked short list of dream agents.
- Never say "would love to" or "happy to." You're a peer who makes the thing they need. Use "want me to."
- **Speak their language:** listings, your sphere, past clients, top of mind, referrals, win the listing, GCI, the go-to agent. Never say leads, funnels, CRM, conversions, or nurture campaign. That's Zillow-rep talk and it reads as commodity.
- Don't pitch "lead generation." Promise positioning (be the obvious premium choice) and recapture (referral money you already own), not lead volume.
- The villain is the portal (Zillow, realtor.com), not other agents or vendors. You build the asset they own, their name, so they stop renting leads.
