Sales Idea Bank · Real Estate Agents

Not a script. A bank of good ideas, lines, and moves.

Pull what fits the agent in front of you. What we sell, the ideas worth pitching, the lines that show value and earn trust, and how to steer a live one to a booked call. The average Atlanta agent closed ~10 deals last year and nets a median ~$25K. They get pitched every day, so generic dies on contact. This is the ammo that doesn't.

The whole game in one line

We don't sell agents a website. We sell them the deals they're already leaving in voicemail, the listings they lose because they look like everyone else, and the sphere they never work. Lead with the leak, not the product.

📦What we're actually selling

One system, three doors in. Every door is a place their money leaks before they ever feel it. You don't pitch all three, you diagnose the agent, walk through the one door that's bleeding, and expand later once they trust you.

The offer (say it plain, only when they ask price)
$750 to build First 30 days on us $1K / month after 3-month minimum

Proof (keep it honest): we doubled a local Atlanta spot's online revenue in about 3 months, and we run the outreach + retention system for an ecommerce brand end to end. Never imply either was a realtor. The free first month is the whole trust play, it's how we can reach out cold and not feel weird about it. Lead the close with it, never the price. The actual proof links live in the Proof kit.

💡The idea bank — things worth pitching

Each of these is a good idea you can float. It plugs a specific leak and comes with a line to pitch it. Pick the one or two that match what you see on their site and listings. Tap copy to grab the pitch line, swap the [brackets] for real details.

🩹Their pain — in their own words

Real agent quotes. Don't recite these at them, feel the emotion behind each one and say it back in your own words. Naming their pain before they do is what turns you from a stranger into someone who gets it.

"Sounds like you've been burned by lead companies before" lands harder than any stat you could quote. Mirror the feeling, then offer the idea that fixes it.

🎬Sample call — the structure, live

One full call from cold open to booked walkthrough, so you can see the six beats in motion. A sample, not a recital, steal the shape, not the words. Notice you pitch ONE leak and never explain the full fix, that's what the meeting is for.

You1 · Open
Hey, is this [Name]? I know you weren't expecting my call, so I'll be quick, did I catch you at a bad time?
Them
Kind of. What's this about?
You2 · Anchor
Fair enough. I was looking at your listing on [street] this morning, the presentation's clean. I run the marketing side for a couple Atlanta agents.
You3 · One question
Real quick, where are most of your deals coming from right now, portals, referrals, your sphere?
Them
Mostly Zillow, some referrals.
You4 · Mirror pain
Gotcha, so a good chunk of the month is rented from Zillow. If they throttle you or hike the price, it goes quiet. That the part that nags you, or is it fine?
Them
I mean, yeah. It's expensive and half of them don't even pick up.
You5 · One idea
Right, paying to fight four other agents for the same tire-kicker. Here's the one thing I'd fix first: the leads you already get, you're losing the after-hours ones to voicemail. A missed call can fire a text in seconds so that buyer stays yours instead of dialing the next name. Usually the cheapest deal you add all year.
Them
Okay, that actually happens to me a lot.
You6 · Book it
Let me not explain it half-baked over the phone. Let's set up a proper look, I'll pull up your site and that listing and show you exactly where it's leaking on your own setup, plus what the fix looks like. First 30 days we run it on us, so you're just watching it work. Mornings or late afternoons better for you?
Them
Mornings. Thursday, maybe.
You
Thursday morning it is. I'll send an invite and text to confirm so it doesn't slip. Talk then.
You never fanned out the menu and never tried to close on the phone. You diagnosed one leak, proved you get it, and booked the room. That's the whole job, Suchet runs the walkthrough from there.

🎣Openers — get past the reflex

The first line decides everything. Never open with your name and company or "how are you today," that's the telemarketer tell and you're gone. Open on their world or hand them an easy out.

📈Value lines — show the money

Lines that make the payoff concrete. Agents think in deals and dollars, so frame everything as more listings, more showings, or money you stop losing. Tap to copy.

🤝Trust lines — make us safe to say yes to

They get pitched by vendors every day. These lines separate us from that: we did homework, we de-risk it, we act like a partner not a one-shot closer.

🔑Convince lines — the reframes

Sharp reframes that shift how they see their own situation. Each one turns a shrug into "huh, I hadn't thought about that." Use a question, not a claim.

🛡Objection handling

An objection isn't a no, it's a reflex or a real question in armor. Never argue. Agree first ("totally fair"), then reframe with a question. The green line under each one tells you which line to pull next, so you don't stall after the comeback.

Three real objections in a row means it's not a fit today. Exit clean: "No worries, you clearly have it handled. If the after-hours thing ever bugs you, I'm one text away." A clean exit today is a callback in three months.

📅Reply & book — when they bite

Every line above ends at "want me to walk you through it?" This is what you say when they say yes. Your job isn't to close the deal on this touch, it's to book the walkthrough. Don't let a warm yes die in the inbox.

The booked look is the win, not the sale. Suchet runs the walkthrough and closes from there. Your KPI is conversations booked, so get the time on the calendar while they're warm and confirm it twice to kill no-shows.

✍️Writing & talking tips — the rules of the road

How to say all of the above so it lands. These are the same whether it's a call, a DM, or an email.

    🗣Talk native — say this, not that

    One outsider word ("funnel," "brand awareness," "leads") and you're flagged as another vendor who doesn't get it. Speak the left column. It's the fastest trust hack there is.

    Say this (their language)
    Never say this (outsider tell)
    The biggest tell: outsiders sell leads, traffic, impressions, brand awareness. Agents buy listing appointments → signed listings → closings. Frame everything as more listings and less follow-up they do themselves.

    ⏰When & how to reach them

    Channel by what they are
    • Marketing-minded agent (posts a lot, cares about their brand): Instagram DM. Highest response. Leave a real comment on a recent listing first, then DM a day later.
    • Busy team lead / higher-fit: cold call. Agents are phone people. Wednesday or Thursday, 8:30–11 AM or 4–6 PM.
    • Everyone else / at scale: cold email through Instantly. Wins on a subject line that reads like a colleague and a first line about their listing or farm.
    • Text: follow-up touch only, never the cold opener.
    Timing
    • Best windows: weekday mornings for DMs and email. Calls Wed/Thu, 8:30–11 AM or 4–6 PM.
    • Never: Sunday 1–4 PM (open houses) or Saturday late morning. You'll catch them with clients and burn the contact.
    • "How'd you get my info?" Agents are on the GAMLS public directory. That's the honest answer, it's public.
    • Warm intro is the cheat code. A shared connection lifts response ~70%. If you know anyone in common, that's your first sentence.
    • Log what works. Which idea you led with, which line, whether they bit. Winners and losers feed back into this bank, it compounds.
