The play

The structure that actually works

The banks are raw material. This is the order you run them in. Five beats, each doing a job on the owner's brain, not just filling space. The whole thing drives to one place: a booked call.

  1. Disarm

    Open with a real, curious question, never a pitch. Their guard is up because everyone in their DMs is selling. A genuine question they can answer in three words breaks the pattern and earns the reply.

  2. Make them name the pain

    Your question points at the nerve (the apps, the buried menu, no time) so they say it out loud, not you. People believe what they say, not what you tell them.

  3. Mirror it

    Say their problem back in their own words. "Yeah, that 30% is brutal." Now you're the one person in their inbox who actually gets the business.

  4. Drop the proof

    Bando, same situation, here's what happened. A real local spot and a specific number. This is where they feel the money they're leaving on the table.

  5. Book the call

    Not a pitch, an invite. "Let me not explain it half-baked in a DM, let's set up a time and I'll show you on your own setup." The DM's only job is the meeting. You close nothing in an inbox.

Proof bank · the Bando drop

Lines that convert once they're talking

Do not open with these. They land the moment the owner engages, right after they admit the pain. Every one aims at the same finish: book a quick call, then hand to the closer. Pick the angle that matches what they just said.

audience → orders
This is exactly what we did for The Bando here in Atlanta. They had the audience, it just wasn't turning into orders they actually owned. We rebuilt the path from their Instagram straight to ordering on their own site, and it doubled their online revenue in four months.
hard number
A real number: before, 94% of the people who landed on Bando's site left without doing anything. No order, no menu, nothing. We got that down to about 50%, and in the same four months their online revenue doubled.
off the apps' leash
The whole idea is getting you off the apps' leash. Your site, your customers, your margin. Same thing we ran with The Bando down the street. Worth hopping on a quick call so I can walk you through exactly what we changed?
local peer
We're local. We did this for The Bando right here in Atlanta, so it's not some out-of-state agency thing. Worth a quick call to see if it'd work for you? Happy to walk you through it.
soft close
No pressure either way, but if you're open to it, let's set up a quick call. I'll walk you through exactly what we did for Bando and what it'd look like for you. What's your week look like?

Notes

What this asset is for: a top-of-funnel meeting-getter for the sales team. The win is a booked call. Closing and delivery are the rep's next step, not in this playbook.

Locked Bando proof: online revenue doubled in 4 months, and site bounce rate went from 94% to 50% (9 in 10 visitors used to leave without ordering, now about half). Use these two everywhere and keep the wording plain so an owner gets it without the jargon.
