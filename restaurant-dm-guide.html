A bank of lines that don't read as a pitch.

Pick, mix, steal. Openers that earn the tap, proof lines that convert once they're talking, and the words that keep you sounding like a real person instead of another agency in the requests folder.

This bank has one job: book the meeting, then hand off to close. The spine: open with a soft, no-pitch question (survive the folder), sell the outcome once they reply (more margin, more orders, more revenue), let Bando prove it, book a quick call. Never open with the outcome. That reads as a pitch and dies in the folder.

The whole opener, and the outcome it sells

No pitch on the surface, ends in an easy reply. Tag shows the nerve it pulls; "outcome sold" is the money result you're actually selling (the website or social is just how you deliver it).

ARun firstthe app cut
random owner-to-owner q, when someone orders you on doordash do you actually keep much of that or is it basically just to get discovered? always wondered how much that cut hurts spots your size
Why it landsThe nerve they feel most, and nobody asks it out loud. Owners rant about the apps. Reads like a peer, not a seller.
Outcome soldKeep the 30% you hand DoorDash. Direct ordering on your own site is how.
Bfood vs website
your food looks genuinely unreal 😅 tried to actually order and kinda got bounced to doordash, couldn't find a straight menu. is that just me or do people tell you that?
Why it landsIt's true and it's a real experience, not flattery. "is that just me?" is almost impossible to answer with a heart.
Outcome soldMore orders that actually land. Stop losing the 94% who bounce (new site is the vehicle).
Cno time
whoever runs this page deserves a raise, it's actually good 😂 is that you or do you have someone doing it? asking bc most spots this size don't have the time and it shows. yours doesn't
Why it landsNames the "Instagram is exhausting" truth, flatters the real human, confirms who you're talking to.
Outcome soldMore covers from the audience you already have (we run the content).
Dslow nights
random q, when you have a slow tuesday do you have a way to just ping your regulars, or is it mostly hope people walk in? curious how spots your size handle it
Why it landsTalks covers and regulars in their language. A dead Tuesday is a real, felt problem.
Outcome soldFill the dead nights with regulars you already have (owned email/SMS is the tool).
Proof bank · the Bando drop

Lines that convert once they're talking

Do not open with these. They land the moment the owner engages, right after they admit the pain. Every one aims at the same finish: book a quick call, then hand to the closer. Pick the angle that matches what they just said.

audience → orders
this is literally what we did for The Bando here in atlanta. they had the audience, it just wasn't turning into orders they actually owned. we rebuilt the path from their instagram straight to ordering on their own site and doubled their online revenue in 4 months
hard number
real number: before, 94% of people who landed on Bando's site left without doing anything, no order, no menu, nothing. we got that down to about 50%. same 4 months their online revenue doubled
off the apps' leash
the whole play is getting you off the apps' leash. your site, your customers, your margin. same thing we ran with The Bando down the street. worth a quick 10 min call to walk you through exactly what we changed?
local peer
we're local, did this for The Bando right here in atlanta so it's not some out-of-state agency thing. worth a quick 10 min to see if it'd work for you? happy to hop on a call and walk you through it
soft close
no pressure either way, but if you're open to it let's grab 10 min on a call. i'll walk you through exactly what we did for Bando and what it'd look like for yall. when's good this week?

Notes

What this asset is for: a top-of-funnel meeting-getter for the sales team. The win is a booked call. Closing and delivery are the rep's next step, not in this playbook.

Locked Bando proof: online revenue doubled in 4 months, and site bounce rate went from 94% to 50% (9 in 10 visitors used to leave without ordering, now about half). Use these two everywhere and keep the wording plain so an owner gets it without the jargon.
